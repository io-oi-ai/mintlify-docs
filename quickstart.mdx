---

This guide walks you through everything you need to get your first Io Ai documentation site live. By the end, you'll have a published document accessible at a public URL and a clear picture of how Io Ai works day to day.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up and create your account">
    Go to [io-oi.ai](https://io-oi.ai) and click **Get started**. You can sign up with a Google account or an email address and password.

    After you sign up, Io Ai prompts you to create your first project. Give your project a name — this is also used as the default subdomain for your published docs (for example, `my-project.io-oi.ai`).

    <Tip>
      You can rename your project and change its subdomain later from **Project settings**. Pick whatever name makes sense right now.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first project">
    If you skipped project creation during sign-up, click **New project** from your dashboard. Fill in:

    - **Name** — a short, descriptive title for this documentation site
    - **Visibility** — choose **Private** while you're getting started; switch to **Public** when you're ready to share

    Click **Create project**. Io Ai opens your new project and shows you an empty document list.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write your first document">
    Click **New document** inside your project. Give the document a title, then start typing in the editor.

    To use the AI assistant, select any text you've written and click **Improve with AI**, or type `/ai` on a blank line to open the prompt panel. Describe what you want — for example, "Rewrite this in plain language" or "Add a step-by-step introduction" — and the assistant drafts the content for you.

    <Note>
      The AI assistant works best when you give it specific instructions. Instead of "make this better", try "simplify this for a non-technical audience" or "break this into numbered steps".
    </Note>

    You can also write directly in Markdown or MDX. Io Ai renders a live preview alongside the editor so you always see what your readers will see.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Invite a collaborator (optional)">
    If you're working with a team, add them now. Open **Project settings** and go to the **Members** tab. Enter your collaborator's email address and choose a role:

    - **Editor** — can create, edit, and publish documents
    - **Viewer** — can read all documents in the project, including unpublished drafts

    Your collaborator receives an email invitation and can join as soon as they accept it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish and share your docs">
    When you're happy with your document, click **Publish** in the top-right corner of the editor. Io Ai immediately makes that document live at your project's URL.

    To share your entire project, click **Share** from your project dashboard. Copy the link and send it to your audience.

    If you want to use your own domain instead of the default Io Ai subdomain, see the [custom domain guide](/configuration/custom-domain).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using the API to create a document

You can also create and update documents programmatically using the Io Ai REST API. First, generate an API key from **Account settings → API keys**. Then send a `POST` request to create a document:

```bash
curl --request POST \
  --url https://api.io-oi.ai/v1/documents \
  --header "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \
  --header "Content-Type: application/json" \
  --data '{
    "project_id": "proj_abc123",
    "title": "My first document",
    "content": "# Hello\n\nThis document was created via the API."
  }'
```

Replace `YOUR_API_KEY` with the key you generated and `proj_abc123` with your project's ID (visible in **Project settings → General**).

A successful request returns a `201` response with the new document's `id`, `title`, and `url`.

<Tip>
  Store your API key in an environment variable rather than hardcoding it. Use `export IO_AI_KEY=your_key_here` and reference it as `$IO_AI_KEY` in your scripts.
</Tip>
