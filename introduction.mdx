Io Ai is a documentation platform built around one idea: your users should be able to read and understand your docs without needing a glossary. Whether you're writing API references, user guides, or internal runbooks, Io Ai's AI assistant helps you get the words right — and its publishing tools get those words in front of the right people.

## Who Io Ai is for

Io Ai is designed for product teams, developer advocates, and technical writers who need to produce documentation that non-technical readers can follow. If your current docs are too dense, too inconsistent, or simply take too long to write and maintain, Io Ai is built to solve those problems.

## What problems Io Ai solves

Most documentation fails for the same reasons: it's written too quickly, reviewed too rarely, and never rewritten when the product changes. Io Ai addresses each of these:

- **Slow writing** — The AI assistant drafts and rewrites content so you spend time refining rather than starting from a blank page.
- **Inconsistent voice** — Plain-language suggestions keep your tone consistent across every document and contributor.
- **Outdated content** — Version history, collaborator roles, and one-click publishing make it easy to keep docs current.

## How Io Ai is organized

Understanding the product model makes everything else easier.

**Projects** are the top-level containers in Io Ai. Each project has its own members, settings, and publishing configuration. Think of a project as a single documentation site — for example, a user guide for one of your products.

**Documents** live inside projects. A document is a single page of content written in Markdown or MDX. Documents have version history, so you can see what changed and who changed it. When you're ready for a document to be visible to your audience, you publish it.

**Publishing** is the act of making a project's documents live. You can publish to a Io Ai subdomain (for example, `your-project.io-oi.ai`) or to a custom domain you control. Publishing is per-project, so you can have a staging project and a production project side by side.

<Note>
  A document only becomes visible to your audience after you publish it. Drafts are always private to your project members.
</Note>
