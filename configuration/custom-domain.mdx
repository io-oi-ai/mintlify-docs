---
title: "Set up a custom domain for your Io Ai docs site"
description: "Connect a domain you own to your Io Ai docs site so visitors reach your documentation at your own branded URL instead of io-oi.ai."
---

By default, your Io Ai docs site is served from a subdomain of `io-oi.ai`. Connecting a custom domain replaces that address with one you control — for example, `docs.yourcompany.com` — so your documentation feels like a natural part of your product. This guide walks you through every step, from adding the domain in Io Ai to verifying that it is live.

<Tip>
  Use a dedicated subdomain like `docs.yourcompany.com` rather than your root domain (`yourcompany.com`). Subdomains are easier to configure with a CNAME record and do not interfere with your marketing site or email delivery.
</Tip>

## Prerequisites

Before you start, confirm that you have:

- A domain name that you own and control.
- Access to your domain's DNS settings (through your registrar or a DNS provider such as Cloudflare, Route 53, or Namecheap).
- **Owner** role in the Io Ai project you want to connect the domain to.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the custom domain in project settings">
    Open your project in Io Ai, go to **Project settings → Custom domain**, and click **Add custom domain**. Enter the full domain or subdomain you want to use (for example, `docs.yourcompany.com`) and click **Save**.

    Io Ai displays the CNAME target you need to add to your DNS records in the next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a CNAME record in your DNS settings">
    Log in to your DNS provider and create a new **CNAME** record using the values below. The exact field labels vary by provider but the values are the same everywhere.

    | Field | Value |
    |-------|-------|
    | **Type** | `CNAME` |
    | **Name / Host** | `docs` (or whatever subdomain you chose; use `@` for a root domain) |
    | **Value / Points to** | `custom.io-oi.ai` |
    | **TTL** | `3600` (or your provider's default) |

    If you are connecting a root domain (for example, `yourcompany.com` rather than `docs.yourcompany.com`), check whether your DNS provider supports CNAME flattening or ALIAS records, which are required for root domains.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for DNS propagation">
    <Warning>
      DNS changes can take up to **48 hours** to propagate worldwide. During this window, some visitors may still reach the old address. Do not delete or modify the CNAME record while propagation is in progress.
    </Warning>

    In most cases propagation completes within 1–2 hours. You can check the current status of your DNS record using a tool such as [dnschecker.org](https://dnschecker.org) or by running the following command in your terminal:

    ```bash
    dig CNAME docs.yourcompany.com +short
    ```

    The output should show `custom.io-oi.ai.` once your record has propagated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the domain in Io Ai">
    Return to **Project settings → Custom domain** in Io Ai and click **Verify domain**. Io Ai checks that the CNAME record resolves correctly. When verification succeeds, your domain status changes to **Active** and your docs site begins serving from your custom domain.

    If verification fails, double-check that the CNAME value exactly matches `custom.io-oi.ai` and that there are no conflicting A records or proxies blocking the lookup.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your SSL certificate">
    Io Ai automatically provisions a free SSL certificate for your custom domain using Let's Encrypt. Certificate issuance usually completes within a few minutes of domain verification. You do not need to upload a certificate or configure anything manually.

    Once the certificate is issued, your site is accessible over HTTPS and all HTTP requests are redirected automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

If your domain does not connect as expected, work through the checks below.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Verification keeps failing in Io Ai">
    This usually means the CNAME record has not propagated yet, or the record was entered incorrectly. Confirm:

    1. The **Name / Host** field contains only the subdomain label (for example, `docs`), not the full domain (`docs.yourcompany.com`). Some registrars add the root domain automatically.
    2. The **Value** field is exactly `custom.io-oi.ai` with no trailing characters or spaces.
    3. There is no conflicting A record or AAAA record for the same subdomain. If one exists, delete it before adding the CNAME.

    After correcting the record, wait 10–15 minutes and click **Verify domain** again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The site loads but shows a certificate warning">
    A certificate warning means the SSL certificate has not been issued yet. This can happen if you visit the domain within the first few minutes after verification. Wait up to 10 minutes and refresh. If the warning persists after 30 minutes, contact [Io Ai support](mailto:support@io-oi.ai) with your domain name.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I'm using Cloudflare and the domain won't verify">
    If your domain is proxied through Cloudflare (orange cloud icon), the CNAME target is masked by Cloudflare's IP addresses, which prevents Io Ai from verifying it. Set the Cloudflare proxy status for the record to **DNS only** (grey cloud) before running verification. Once Io Ai verifies and your SSL certificate is issued, you can re-enable the Cloudflare proxy if you prefer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I want to use my root domain, not a subdomain">
    Root domain configuration requires either CNAME flattening (supported by Cloudflare and AWS Route 53) or an ALIAS record (supported by some registrars). If your DNS provider does not support either, you will not be able to use the root domain directly. We recommend using `docs.yourcompany.com` instead. Contact your DNS provider's support to confirm what record types are available for root domains.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
