Io Ai is built for teams. You can invite colleagues to a project, edit documents together in real time, leave inline comments, and run structured review workflows before publishing. This guide covers everything you need to work on documentation with your team.

## Invite team members to a project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open project settings">
    From your project dashboard, click **Settings** in the left sidebar, then select **Team**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the email address">
    Type the email address of the person you want to invite. You can add multiple addresses at once, separated by commas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a role">
    Choose the role for each invitee: **Editor** or **Viewer**. See the roles reference below for details on what each role can do.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Send invite**. The invitee receives an email with a link to join the project. They need an Io Ai account — if they don't have one, the invitation link guides them through sign-up.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Collaborator roles

Every project member has one of three roles. The project **Owner** assigns and changes roles for all other members.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Owner" icon="crown" defaultOpen={false}>
    The Owner has full control over the project. There is exactly one Owner per project.

    **Owners can:**
    - Create, edit, publish, and delete any document
    - Invite and remove collaborators
    - Change any collaborator's role
    - Configure project settings, including the custom domain and access control
    - Transfer ownership to another team member
    - Delete the project

    <Note>
    Only the Owner can access billing settings, change the project's subdomain, or delete the project. These settings are not available to Editors or Viewers.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Editor" icon="pen" defaultOpen={false}>
    Editors can write and manage content but cannot change project settings or manage team membership.

    **Editors can:**
    - Create, edit, and delete documents
    - Publish and unpublish documents
    - Create and manage folders
    - Leave and resolve comments
    - Participate in review workflows

    **Editors cannot:**
    - Invite or remove collaborators
    - Change project settings
    - Access billing
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Viewer" icon="eye" defaultOpen={false}>
    Viewers have read-only access to the project's content in the dashboard. They cannot make changes.

    **Viewers can:**
    - Read all documents in the project dashboard (including drafts)
    - Leave comments on documents
    - View review and approval status

    **Viewers cannot:**
    - Edit or create documents
    - Publish or unpublish documents
    - Change project settings

    Viewer access is useful for stakeholders, subject-matter experts, or reviewers who need to read and comment without editing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Co-edit documents in real time

Multiple Editors can work on the same document at the same time. Io Ai shows each collaborator's cursor position and highlights their changes as they type, so you can see who is editing what without refreshing.

To start co-editing:

1. Open the document you want to work on.
2. Share the document link with your teammate and ask them to open it.
3. Both of you will see each other's cursor in the editor and changes appear live.

Io Ai automatically merges simultaneous edits. If two people edit the same sentence at the same time, the platform resolves the conflict and preserves both contributions where possible.

## Comment on documents

Inline comments let you leave feedback on specific text without editing the document itself. This is useful for review rounds, questions, or flagging sections that need more work.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the text">
    Highlight the word, sentence, or paragraph you want to comment on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a comment">
    A comment icon appears in the margin. Click it, or right-click the selection and choose **Add comment**. Type your feedback and press **Submit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reply to comments">
    Other team members can reply directly in the comment thread. Use replies to discuss, clarify, or agree on changes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolve comments">
    Once the issue is addressed, click **Resolve** on the comment. Resolved comments are hidden from the default view but remain in the comment history if you need to reference them later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Review and approval workflows

For teams that need a formal sign-off before publishing, Io Ai includes a review workflow.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request a review">
    When your document is ready for review, click **Request review** in the editor toolbar. Select one or more team members as reviewers. They receive a notification by email and in the Io Ai dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reviewer reads and comments">
    The reviewer opens the document, reads the content, and leaves inline comments on anything that needs changes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Author addresses feedback">
    The original author edits the document in response to comments, resolving them as each point is addressed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reviewer approves">
    Once the reviewer is satisfied, they click **Approve**. The document is marked as approved in the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish">
    With approval in place, click **Publish** to make the document live. See [Publish your documentation](/guides/publishing) for the full publishing workflow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can set a project-wide policy that requires at least one approval before a document can be published. Find this option in **Project settings → Review policy**.
</Tip>

## Remove a collaborator

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open project settings">
    Go to **Settings → Team** in your project dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the collaborator">
    Locate the team member you want to remove in the members list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove them">
    Click the **...** menu next to their name and select **Remove from project**. Confirm the action.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The removed collaborator loses access to the project immediately. Their past comments and edit history remain in the project.

<Note>
Only the Owner can remove collaborators. If you need to remove someone and you are not the Owner, contact your project's Owner directly.
</Note>
