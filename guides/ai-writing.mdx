Io Ai includes an AI writing assistant built directly into the document editor. You can use it to generate a first draft, rewrite existing content in plain language, simplify technical jargon, expand a brief outline into full sections, or produce summaries and introductions. The assistant works in context — it understands the document you're editing and responds to your instructions.

<Note>
Always review AI-generated content before publishing. The assistant can save significant time, but it may occasionally misstate technical details, miss important nuance, or produce phrasing that doesn't match your product exactly. A quick review pass ensures accuracy and keeps your documentation trustworthy.
</Note>

## Activate the AI assistant

The AI assistant is available in every document editor. Open any document and look for the **Ask AI** button in the toolbar, or press `Cmd+K` (macOS) / `Ctrl+K` (Windows, Linux) from anywhere in the editor to open the AI command palette.

## Generate a first draft

If you're starting with a blank page or a rough outline, the AI assistant can build a complete first draft for you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a new or existing document">
    Create a new document or open one that contains an outline or topic notes you want to expand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the AI command palette">
    Press `Cmd+K` / `Ctrl+K` or click **Ask AI** in the toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe what you want">
    Type a prompt that describes the document. Be specific: include the topic, the audience, and the goal. For example:

    > Write a how-to guide explaining how a non-technical user can reset their password in our web app. Include numbered steps and a tip about what to do if the reset email doesn't arrive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the draft">
    The assistant inserts the generated content into the editor. Read through it, adjust any details that don't match your product, and refine the language as needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
The more specific your prompt, the better the output. Tell the AI who the reader is, what they need to accomplish, and any constraints or conventions to follow (for example, "use numbered steps" or "keep sentences under 20 words").
</Tip>

## Rewrite content in plain language

If you have existing documentation that's too technical or hard to read, the AI assistant can rewrite it in plain language without losing the meaning.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the text you want to rewrite">
    Highlight the sentence, paragraph, or section in the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the AI command palette">
    Press `Cmd+K` / `Ctrl+K` with text selected, or right-click and choose **Rewrite with AI**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Rewrite in plain language">
    Select the **Rewrite in plain language** option, or type a custom instruction such as:

    > Rewrite this for a reader with no technical background. Replace jargon with everyday language.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accept or refine">
    Review the rewritten version. Click **Accept** to replace the original text, or ask the assistant to adjust further before accepting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Simplify, expand, or restructure text

Beyond full rewrites, you can ask the assistant to make targeted changes to selected text.

| What you want | Example prompt |
|---|---|
| Simplify | "Make this shorter and easier to understand." |
| Expand | "Expand this into a full paragraph with an example." |
| Restructure | "Convert this paragraph into a numbered step-by-step list." |
| Adjust tone | "Make this more direct and action-oriented." |
| Fix clarity | "Rewrite this so the main point comes first." |

Select the relevant text, open the AI command palette, and type your instruction.

## Generate a summary or introduction

Io Ai can write summaries and introductory paragraphs for documents that already have content.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the document">
    Navigate to the document you want to summarize or add an introduction to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place your cursor">
    Click at the beginning of the document (for an introduction) or at the end (for a summary), or select text you want summarized.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask the AI">
    Open the command palette and use a prompt like:

    > Write a one-paragraph introduction for this document. Explain what the reader will learn and who this guide is for.

    Or for a summary:

    > Write a two-sentence summary of this document suitable for a table of contents description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and insert">
    Check the output for accuracy and insert it into the document.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Review and accept AI suggestions

When the AI assistant produces output, it appears inline in the editor as a suggestion. You have three options:

- **Accept** — Replace the selected text or insert the suggestion at the cursor position.
- **Discard** — Remove the suggestion and keep the original content.
- **Refine** — Keep the prompt open and add a follow-up instruction to adjust the output before accepting.

You can also accept part of a suggestion by manually copying the sections you want and dismissing the rest.

## Tips for better prompts

- **Give context.** Include who the reader is and what they're trying to do. "Explain this to a developer using our REST API" produces different output than "Explain this to a new user."
- **Be specific about format.** Mention if you want numbered steps, bullet points, a table, or prose.
- **Set a length expectation.** "Write a concise two-sentence summary" or "Write a detailed explanation with examples" helps the AI match your intent.
- **Iterate.** If the first output isn't right, refine it with a follow-up instruction rather than starting over.
- **Use your own words first.** Even rough notes or an outline give the AI much better context than a blank page.
